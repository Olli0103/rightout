# RightOut v0.10.0

RightOut 0.10.0 makes market, jurisdiction, provider authority, and outcome
evidence separate runtime contracts. It expands the catalog and human
preference workflows without turning legal rights, government portals, browser
signals, or technical capability into unearned authority or deletion claims.

## Highlights

- Machine-readable readiness for 11 market groups, including official-source
  review dates, exact implemented support, safe defaults, and visible gaps.
- Market-policy binding across campaign approval, durable workers, controller
  requests, browser forms, and all 22 normalized parity routes. Unsupported or
  review-due rights stop before profile SecretRefs or provider I/O.
- One separate UK controller route, `cognism_uk`, with UK-only eligibility,
  request wording, identity handling, rights digest, and conservative
  one-calendar-month recheck semantics.
- Authorized-canary schema v2 with identity precision, recall and accuracy,
  scoped outcome timing, broker coverage, and authorization/deployment evidence
  digests. Real-deployment effectiveness remains `needs_evidence` until
  consistent authorized facts exist.
- Human-only California DROP filing and status tracking bound to the exact
  fresh official registry snapshot, the August 1, 2026 processing phase, the
  ordinary 90-day window, and the 45-day broker cycle.
- Human-observed Global Privacy Control state for a browser setting or
  extension. It records a sale/sharing preference only; RightOut does not
  configure the browser, contact a site, or treat GPC as deletion proof.
- Deeply immutable policy contracts and revision-bound DROP/GPC approvals so
  in-process mutation, registry drift, or out-of-order concurrent observations
  fail closed.
- A schema-v7 catalog with 57 reviewed records, 56 code-enforced scan lanes,
  29 executable email/removal targets, and a 52-tool OpenClaw manifest.

## Preserved boundaries

- The pinned Unbroker technical baseline remains exact at 22/22 normalized
  contracts: 20 form, one email, and one phone route.
- Current public provider evidence authorizes zero default live form routes;
  eight routes prohibit automation and 14 remain `needs_evidence`.
- Search-index discovery is not identity proof. SMTP acceptance, a portal
  status, GPC, and a submitted request are not confirmed deletion.
- DROP identity verification and portal submission remain human actions.
- Unknown and conditional markets remain human-only; RightOut does not infer
  residence or legal rights from IP address, locale, or model knowledge.
- Custom-target provider execution, hosted multi-tenancy, managed human
  operations, and universal/permanent deletion claims remain out of scope.

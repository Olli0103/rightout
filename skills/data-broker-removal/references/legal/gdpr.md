# GDPR / UK GDPR Notes

Use only as operational context, not legal advice.

- Relevant concept: GDPR / DSGVO Article 17 erasure of personal data where the subject is eligible and the controller is in scope.
- Article 17 grounds include: data is no longer necessary, consent is withdrawn and no other legal basis applies, the subject objects and no overriding legitimate grounds apply, data was unlawfully processed, erasure is required by EU/member-state law, or child information-society-service data is involved.
- Article 17 has exceptions, including expression/information, legal obligations/public authority, public health, research/archiving/statistics, and legal claims. Do not promise guaranteed erasure.
- Controllers generally have one month to respond to data-subject requests, starting from receipt of the request or from receipt of needed identity information.
- Requests must be honest about jurisdiction and should disclose only the minimum data required for identification.
- Data on a third-party record can still be personal data about the subject when the subject's email, phone, name, or association is exposed; handle it as targeted `delete my personal data` rather than acting on the third party's whole record.
- Proof of identity may be requested, but OpenClaw must not volunteer passport, national ID, full birth date, or other high-risk identifiers unless the subject explicitly approves that disclosure and the controller has explained why it is necessary.

OpenClaw rules:

- Do not cite or file GDPR/UK GDPR/DSGVO requests unless eligibility and controller scope are plausible and the operator explicitly approves the submission class.
- Prefer the controller's official privacy contact, DPO contact, or rights portal. Do not use third-party blog-sourced email addresses unless independently verified from the controller.
- Keep the evidence set narrow: source/listing URL, field names disclosed, request kind, and confirmation evidence. Do not store identity-document images in the skill ledger.
- Mark outcomes precisely: requested, awaiting controller response, rejected/needs identity, confirmed removed, residual lawful-public-record exposure, or inconclusive.
